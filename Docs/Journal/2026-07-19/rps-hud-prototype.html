Portrait, like the real app: your camp at the bottom, the enemy's at the top of a scrolling field. Units are the literal objects — rock, scissors, wrapping paper, and a miner's pickaxe (the currency is gold) — rendered as bare alpha-cutout silhouettes tinted in team colour, so each type reads by shape alone. The finish is dark and machined: graphite panels, hairline edges, one gold accent, subtle gradients — built to read as fast and precise, not cartoony.

Plate anatomy

50 3× b a c
  1. aStack count — queued units of this type, on a skewed gold tag; hidden at zero.
  2. bGold cost — always visible; the number turns red and the plate dims when unaffordable.
  3. cBuild progress — gold fill, time until the next unit of this type spawns.

The icon is the unit sprite at button scale — no separate button art, and the eye maps plate to field instantly. The four plates split the bottom edge evenly; the resource column stacks up the bottom-left corner: gold first, then the live army count per type.

Style rules

  • Dark, machined surfaces. Graphite panels with a one-pixel edge and a single top highlight — the "dashboard at night" read. No parchment, no wood, no rounded blobs.
  • Sharp geometry. 2–3 px corner radii, faceted glyphs, skewed count tags. Curves are reserved for things that are actually round (scissor rings, the paper roll).
  • One accent, spent on value. Gold marks everything worth something: the currency, costs, build progress, stack tags. Nothing else gets to be gold.
  • Subtle gradients only. Every gradient is the base colour ±10–30% lightness — sheen, not rainbow. On the field, team identity is the tint of the silhouette itself.
  • Motion sells performance. Flick-scroll with momentum, instant plate feedback, tokens that glide at 60 fps — the UI itself should feel like the low-latency claim.
